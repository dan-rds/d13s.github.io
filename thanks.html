I wanted to say thank you, not only for the opportunity but also for the experience and knowledge I gained during my Internship at the SETI Institute.
Over the summer I was given the opportunity to work on upgrading SETI’s computer network at the Allen Telescope Array. Throughout the summer I built a ‘network boot’ server that will allow any computer on the network to boot, not from the hard drive but from the network. This increases the cohesion of the system(especially interchangeability and upgradability) and decreases the time spent in trouble shooting/debugging the system. For those fluent in computer-see, I chronicled this project and wrote a how-to guide for others.
Although I initially had little experience with networking, by the end on my summer internship, I was able to aid Matt Lebofsky at Breakthrough Listen with his quest to network boot.
I have learned invaluable skills and lessons during this summer and I look forward to putting them to use thought the rest of my career.
Sincerely,
Daniel Richards ddrichar@ucsc.edu
